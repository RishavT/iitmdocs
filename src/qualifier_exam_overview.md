# Qualifier Exam Preparation

All regular entry applicants must go through the 4-week Qualifier process to earn admission to the Foundation Level.

## Course Content by Program

### BS in Data Science and Applications (DS) and BS in Management and Data Science (MG)
On the start date of the Qualifier process Week-1, access to the content for the four Foundation level courses will be provided:
1. English 1
2. Maths-1
3. Statistics-1
4. Computational Thinking

### BS in Electronic Systems (ES) and BS in Aeronautics and Space Technology (AE)
On the start date of the Qualifier process Week-1, access to the content for the four Foundation level courses will be provided:
1. English 1
2. Mathematics-1
3. Electronic Systems Thinking and Circuits
4. Introduction to C Programming

## How Content is Released

- Content is released week-wise over the 4-week period
- Content includes:
  - Videos
  - Tutorials
  - Practice assignments
  - Graded assignments
  - Text transcripts
- Content will be made live with an announcement on the portal and an alert through email
- Every week the graded assignment must be submitted in each course

## Registration and Week-1 Access

- All applicants registering for the Qualifier Exam will receive access to Week-1 sample course content
- This allows candidates to explore the learning material and understand the structure of the program before attempting the exam
- To receive access, learners must complete the Qualifier Exam registration form available on the official website: https://study.iitm.ac.in/
- Access to the Week-1 content will be provided after successful submission of the form

# FAQs

**Question**: What exactly is the qualifier exam?
**Answer**: The qualifier exam is a 4-hour exam conducted after 4 weeks of online classes and assignments.
It tests only the content taught during those 4 weeks.
Clearing the qualifier with the required cut-offs makes a learner eligible to register for Foundation-level courses in the IIT Madras BS program.



**Question**: Why is the qualifier exam required?
**Answer**: The qualifier exam is mandatory for regular entry applicants.
It ensures that learners meet the minimum academic readiness required to enter the Foundation level of the program.



**Question**: Is the qualifier exam the same for all programs?
**Answer**: No.
The syllabus differs by program:

* BS in Data Science and Applications and BS in Management and Data Science: English 1, Maths-1, Statistics-1, Computational Thinking
* BS in Electronic Systems and BS in Aeronautics and Space Technology: English 1, Mathematics-1, Electronic Systems Thinking and Circuits, Introduction to C Programming



**Question**: Is the qualifier exam competitive or only qualifying?
**Answer**: The qualifier exam is primarily a qualifying exam, not a competitive one.
Candidates must meet minimum subject-wise and overall cut-offs to pass.



**Question**: Does clearing the qualifier guarantee admission?
**Answer**: Yes.
Clearing the qualifier guarantees admission to the Foundation level provided there are no documentation issues.



**Question**: Who conducts the qualifier exam?
**Answer**: The qualifier exam is conducted by the IIT Madras BS program administration.



**Question**: Is Mathematics compulsory to attempt the qualifier?
**Answer**: Mathematics is not compulsory as an advanced school subject.
The program is designed to be inclusive, and the qualifier tests only what is taught during the 4-week coursework, regardless of prior background.



**Question**: Is the qualifier easier or harder than end-term exams?
**Answer**: The qualifier is generally considered moderately difficult but easier than regular term exams, provided the learner stays consistent with lectures and assignments.



**Question**: How much time should I prepare for the qualifier?
**Answer**: The qualifier spans 4 weeks of coursework, with an expected engagement of about 10 hours per week per course.
Consistent daily preparation is recommended.



**Question**: Is coaching required to clear the qualifier?
**Answer**: No.
Coaching is not required. Learners can clear the qualifier through self-study using lectures, assignments, and practice material provided by IIT Madras.



**Question**: Can I clear the qualifier with self-study?
**Answer**: Yes.
The qualifier is fully based on the 4 weeks of provided content, and many students clear it through consistent self-study.



**Question**: Does the qualifier test coding skills?
**Answer**: No.
The qualifier exam tests Computational Thinking, which covers basic pseudocode, flowcharts, boolean logic, and simple algorithms.
It does not test advanced coding skills or programming in any specific language.



**Question**: Is English compulsory in the qualifier exam?
**Answer**: Yes.
English 1 is one of the four compulsory subjects in the qualifier exam for all programs.



**Question**: What is the most common reason students fail the qualifier?
**Answer**: Common reasons include:

* Not meeting individual subject-wise and overall cut-offs, as a minimum eligibility score in each subject based on category is mandatory
* Inadequate preparation during the 4-week study period
* Absence from the exam

Students can improve their chances by thoroughly understanding the course content and completing all assignments during the preparation period.



**Question**: Is prior coding knowledge required?
**Answer**: No.
The program starts from basic concepts. However, learners who have never done coding before will need to put in effort and practice to learn.



**Question**: Where can I find the qualifier exam dates?
**Answer**: Qualifier exam dates for each term are published in the Academic Calendar on the official website.
For the latest dates, please refer to: https://study.iitm.ac.in/ds/admissions.html#AD0



**Question**: How and where can I apply for the program?
**Answer**: Anyone who is eligible may apply by filling in the application form, uploading required documents and paying the application fee.

We recommend that you go through the Academics Page to better understand the program and the Admissions Page to understand the admission process before applying.



**Question**: What is the qualifier process?
**Answer**: All applicants will have to go through a Qualifier Process, wherein they will get access to 4 weeks of content for the four foundational level courses - English I, Mathematics for Data Science I, Statistics for Data Science I and Computational Thinking. Check the Qualifier Process in the Admissions page for details.



**Question**: How many times a year will there be admissions? Will it be only once a year?
**Answer**: For now, we are running applications / admissions thrice a year.



**Question**: What are the documents / files required to apply for the qualifier process of the program?
**Answer**: The list of required documents is available on the Application Process section of the admissions page.



**Question**: Can I edit my application form after submitting it?
**Answer**: Your application form will be considered as submitted only upon making the application fee payment. Until then, you may edit the contents of your application form. After the application fee has been paid, you will not be allowed to edit the application form.

In case your uploaded document(s) gets "returned / rejected" during verification, you will be given a chance to re-upload a valid document within a stipulated deadline.



**Question**: My application status says "Verification Under Process". How long will it take for my application to be verified?
**Answer**: Verification of documents may take upto 3 weeks. Please be patient. We will keep you updated about your application status by email.



**Question**: How to avoid getting my application "returned / rejected"?
**Answer**: After you submit your application, we verify the same before changing the status of uploaded document(s) to "accepted" or "returned / rejected".

Be careful when filling the application form and double check the information you enter. We verify the files / documents you have uploaded (photograph, signature, ID card, SC / ST / OBC-NCL / EWS / PwD document if applicable). If any of the files / documents uploaded is unclear or broken or found to be wrong or incorrect, your document may be "returned / rejected".

In case your uploaded document(s) gets "returned / rejected" during verification, you will be given a chance to re-upload a valid document within a stipulated deadline.

Being honest and careful while filling the application form and uploading correct documents in the correct formats will help get your application "accepted". Check the Application Process for required documents, document formats and sizes.
