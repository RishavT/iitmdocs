Topic: Qualifier Exam Format, Mode, Cities, and Exam-Day Logistics



Question: Is the qualifier exam online or offline?
Answer:
* For students within India: the qualifier exam is conducted offline (in person).
* For students outside India: the exam is conducted as a remote proctored exam, subject to availability.



Question: Where is the qualifier exam conducted?
Answer: The exam is conducted at designated exam centres based on the city selected by the learner.
If a centre is not available in a learner’s city (especially for overseas learners), a remote proctored exam may be used.



Question: How long is the qualifier exam?
Answer: The qualifier exam duration is 4 hours (240 minutes).



Question: How many subjects are tested?
Answer:
* BS in Data Science and Applications: Mathematics, Statistics, Computational Thinking, English
* All subjects must be attempted.



Question: What is the question format?
Answer: The qualifier exam includes:

* MCQs
* MSQs
* Numerical questions
* Short-answer questions



Question: Is there negative marking?
Answer: No.
There is no negative marking.



Question: Can I skip a subject?
Answer: No.
All four subjects are mandatory.
Learners must meet both subject-wise and overall cut-offs.



Question: Are all subjects tested on the same day?
Answer: Yes.
All subjects are tested on the same day.



Question: Will there be separate papers?
Answer: The qualifier exam consists of four subjects, attempted as part of the same examination session.



Question: Can I use a calculator?
Answer: Yes.
A calculator is enabled in the exam window.
There is no need to carry a physical calculator.



Question: Is the qualifier exam open book?
Answer: No.
The qualifier exam is a closed-book, in-person, proctored examination.



Question: How are exam cities allocated?
Answer: Exam cities are allocated based on the learner’s selected preference, subject to availability.



Question: Can I choose my exam city?
Answer: Yes.
Learners can select their exam city during the application process.



Question: Can I change my exam city after selection?
Answer: Yes.
An exam city edit window will be provided, and learners will be notified by email when it opens.



Question: Are exam centres available in all states?
Answer: Learners should check the official exam city list published by the IIT Madras BS program to confirm availability.



Question: What documents are required at the exam centre?
Answer: Learners must carry:

* An original photo ID (Aadhaar / PAN / Passport / College ID)
* A photocopy of the hall ticket



Question: Is Aadhaar mandatory?
Answer: No.
Any valid original photo ID (Aadhaar / PAN / Passport / College ID) is acceptable.



Question: What happens if I forget my hall ticket?
Answer: Learners must mandatorily bring the physical hall ticket to the exam centre.



Question: Can I request special accommodation?
Answer: Yes.
Learners requiring special accommodation must email the support team at least two weeks before the exam, along with valid proof.



Question: Is a scribe allowed?
Answer: Yes.
Learners who require a scribe due to physical disability will be contacted by the team before the exam.



Question: Are exam timings flexible for international students?
Answer: No.
Remote-proctored exams for international students follow IST-based timings.



Question: Can I write the exam from home?
Answer:
* Learners in India must write the exam in person.
* Learners outside India may write a remote-proctored exam, depending on centre availability.



Question: Can I come to the IIT Madras campus for exams?
Answer: No.
The IIT Madras BS program is a non-campus program.
Exams are conducted only at designated exam centres, not on the IIT Madras campus.
