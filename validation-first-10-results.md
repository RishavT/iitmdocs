**Bot Answer:**

The IIT Madras BS programme distinguishes between a "reattempt" and a "reapply" for the Qualifier Exam based on a student's previous status and the timing.

Here's the breakdown:

**Reattempt Qualifier:**
*   **Eligibility:** This option is for candidates who were eligible to appear for the first Qualifier Exam in a term but either missed it or failed it.
*   **Process:** They can appear for a second Qualifier Exam within the *same term* (typically after Week 8, following the first exam after Week 4).
*   **Coursework:** They **do not** need to repeat the 4-week coursework or assignments.
*   **Fees:** A reduced reattempt fee is applicable (e.g., Rs. 1500 for General/OBC, Rs. 750 for SC/ST/PwD).

**Reapply Qualifier:**
*   **Eligibility:** This applies to candidates who:
    *   Did not become eligible to appear for the Qualifier Exam in a previous term (e.g., did not meet the minimum assignment score criteria).
    *   Their previous Qualifier score has expired (valid for 3 terms following the exam date).
*   **Process:** They are treated as a "fresh application" in a future term.
*   **Coursework:** They **must** repeat the entire 4-week coursework and qualifying process from the beginning.
*   **Fees:** They must pay the *full qualifier exam fee* again (Rs. 3000 for General category, with applicable waivers).

In essence, a **reattempt** is a second chance within the same application cycle, while **reapplying** means starting the entire qualification process over again in a new cycle.
